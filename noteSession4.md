## 3) Key Content and Takeaways  

### Part 1 — Key Editing Priorities for Edited Book Chapters  
**Why dedicated editing matters**  
- Editors and reviewers form quick judgments from formatting and language quality.  
- Publisher compliance reduces avoidable revision cycles.  
- Consistency matters more in edited volumes to align chapter styles across authors.

**Core editing areas to check**  
- **Formatting:** headings, fonts, spacing, figures and tables (follow the publisher guideline document).  
- **Language and style:** clarity, conciseness, scholarly tone, appropriate active and passive balance.  
- **Consistency:** terminology, abbreviations, tense, spelling conventions (Malaysian or British English).  
- **Referencing:** required style (APA or Vancouver as specified), completeness and correctness.  
- **Overall polish:** remove repetition, vary sentence structure, strengthen conclusions.

**Publisher-specific reminders**  
- Use the uploaded guideline documents as the source of truth.  
- Common rejection reasons include poor formatting, inconsistent style, and language errors.

## 5) Copy-paste Prompts for Editing and Compliance  
Edit bracketed items before use.

 **Prompt 1 – Full draft compliance check**
   ```
   Act as a professional copyeditor familiar with UTM Press guidelines. Review my entire uploaded chapter draft and:
   1. Check adherence to formatting rules (headings, spacing, fonts, etc.).
   2. Flag any inconsistencies in terminology, abbreviations, or referencing style.
   3. Suggest specific fixes with examples.
   Output in a structured list.
   ```

   **Prompt 2 – Language polishing**
   ```
   Review my draft for clarity, conciseness, and academic tone. Identify 5–7 sentences or paragraphs that are wordy, repetitive, or unclear. Provide concise rewritten versions in scholarly Malaysian/British English, preserving my original meaning and voice.
   ```

   **Prompt 3 – Consistency across sections**
   ```
   Analyse my draft for consistency in terminology (e.g., "artificial intelligence" vs "AI"), tense usage, and abbreviation introduction. List all inconsistencies found and suggest standardised forms based on academic best practices.
   ```

   **Prompt 4 – Referencing and citation polish**
   ```
   Check my draft for referencing issues: missing citations, incomplete references, or incorrect formatting. Suggest specific additions or corrections using sources from my uploaded research papers. Also recommend places to strengthen claims with additional evidence.
   ```

   **Prompt 5 – Final readability enhancements**
   ```
   Suggest improvements to sentence variety and flow in [specific section]. Provide 4–5 revised example paragraphs that enhance readability while maintaining formal academic tone.
   ```
